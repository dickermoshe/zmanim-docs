Bain Hashmashos RT 13 Point 24 Degrees¶

The beginning of bein hashmashos according to Rabbeinu Tam, using a degree-based version of the 58.5-minutes-after-sunset calculation.

It is calculated for when the sun is 13.24° below the western geometric horizon after sunset. This is based on the sun’s position 58.5 minutes after sunset in Jerusalem around the equinox / equilux.

Yisrael Vehazmanim Vol. III page 1028, No. 50 says a dip of slightly less than 13° should be used. The documented calculation uses 13.24°, based on 13.2456° truncated to 13.24°, giving a time about 1.5 seconds earlier.

The difference between 13.24° and 13° is listed as 1 minute 10 seconds in Jerusalem during the equinox, 1 minute 29 seconds in Jerusalem during the solstice, and 1 minute 56 seconds in New York during the solstice.

This time may not be available in northern or southern locations, even south of the Arctic Circle and north of the Antarctic Circle, where the sun may not go low enough below the horizon for this calculation.

Bain Hashmashos RT 13 Point 5 Minutes Before 7 Point 083 Degrees¶

The beginning of bein hashmashos according to Rabbeinu Tam in this calculation.

It is calculated as 13.5 minutes, which is ¾ of an 18-minute mil, before shkiah calculated as the sun being 7.083° below the horizon.

This time may not be available in northern or southern locations, even south of the Arctic Circle and north of the Antarctic Circle, where the sun may not go low enough below the horizon for this calculation.

Bain Hashmashos RT 2 Stars¶

The beginning of bein hashmashos according to Rabbeinu Tam, following the opinion of the Divrei Yosef as cited in Yisrael Vehazmanim.

It is calculated by taking 5/18, or 27.77%, of the time between alos, defined here as when the sun is 19.8° before sunrise, and sunrise, and adding that amount of time to sunset.

This time may not be available in northern or southern locations, even south of the Arctic Circle and north of the Antarctic Circle, where the sun may not go low enough below the horizon for this calculation.

Bain Hashmashos RT 58 Point 5 Minutes¶

The beginning of bein hashmashos according to Rabbeinu Tam, calculated as a fixed time after sunset.

It is calculated as 58.5 minutes after sunset. The explanation given is that bein hashmashos is ¾ of a mil before tzais, or 3 ¼ mil after sunset; with a mil of 18 minutes, 3.25 × 18 equals 58.5 minutes.

This time may not be available in places such as the Arctic Circle, where there is at least one day a year when the sun does not rise and one when it does not set.

Bain Hashmashos Yereim 13 Point 5 Minutes¶

The beginning of bein hashmashos, or twilight, according to the Yereim (Rabbi Eliezer of Metz). According to the Yereim as described here, bein hashmashos starts ¾ of a mil before sunset and tzais, or nightfall, starts at sunset.

It is calculated as 13.5 minutes before sunset, which is ¾ of an 18-minute mil.

This time may not be available in places such as the Arctic Circle, where there is at least one day a year when the sun does not rise and one when it does not set.

Bain Hashmashos Yereim 2 Point 1 Degrees¶

The beginning of bein hashmashos according to the Yereim (Rabbi Eliezer of Metz). According to the Yereim as described here, bein hashmashos starts ¾ of a mil before sunset and tzais, or nightfall, starts at sunset.

It is calculated by the sun’s position 2.1° above the horizon around the equinox / equilux in Yerushalayim, corresponding to 13.5 minutes, or ¾ of an 18-minute mil, before sunset.

This time may not be available in places such as the Arctic Circle, where there is at least one day a year when the sun does not rise and one when it does not set.

Bain Hashmashos Yereim 3 Point 05 Degrees¶

The beginning of bain hashmashos, twilight, according to the Yereim (Rabbi Eliezer of Metz). According to the Yereim, bain hashmashos starts ¾ of a mil before sunset, and tzais, nightfall, starts at sunset.

Calculated when the sun is 3.05° above the horizon around the equinox / equilux, corresponding to 18 minutes, or ¾ of a 24-minute mil, before sunset. A refraction value of 0.5166° is used, described as lechumra by about 14 seconds.

The 0.5166° refraction value is described as more in line with actual refraction in Eretz Yisrael and is cited from Rabbi Yedidya Manet in Zmanei HaHalacha Lema'aseh, p. 11. The same refraction is also used by Luach Itim Lebinah.

For more details, see The Yereim's Bain Hashmashos.

This time may not be available when the calculation cannot be computed, such as in the Arctic Circle where there is at least one day a year when the sun does not rise and one when it does not set.
