Sun Transit¶

Solar noon, also called sundial noon, is when the Sun is transiting the celestial meridian. See The Definition of Chatzos for more detail on the definition of solar noon or midday.

Using the NOAA calculation, this is calculated as astronomical noon.

Technical details

Source method: AstronomicalCalendar.getSunTransit

Technical reference: com.kosherjava.zmanim.AstronomicalCalendar.getSunTransit

Fixed Local Chatzos Hayom¶

Fixed local chatzos is a local noon time adjusted from standard time based on the local longitude. This is the time of chatzos according to the Aruch Hashulchan in Orach Chaim 233:14 and Rabbi Moshe Feinstein in Igros Moshe Orach Chaim 1:24 and 2:20.

The globe’s 360° divided by 24 hours gives 15° per hour, or 4 minutes per degree. At longitudes 0°, 15°, 30°, and so on, chatzos is exactly 12:00 noon. Other longitudes are adjusted by their distance from the nearest 15° multiple, at 4 minutes per degree. For example, Lakewood, N.J., at longitude -74.222, is 0.778° from -75°; multiplied by 4, this gives 3 minutes and 7 seconds, for a chatzos of 11:56:53.

This time is not tied to theoretical 15° time zones; it adjusts to the actual time zone and Daylight saving time.

Technical details

Source method: ComprehensiveZmanimCalendar.getFixedLocalChatzosHayom

Technical reference: com.kosherjava.zmanim.ComprehensiveZmanimCalendar.getFixedLocalChatzosHayom

Chatzos Hayom¶

Chatzos hayom is the daytime midpoint used for chatzos. With the NOAA calculation, it can be astronomical chatzos, described as a more accurate solar-noon calculation. See The Definition of Chatzos for a detailed explanation of ways to calculate chatzos.

With the NOAA calculation, chatzos hayom is calculated as astronomical chatzos when the setting to use astronomical chatzos is enabled. If that setting is not enabled, it is calculated as the halfway point between sunrise and sunset.

If the halfway-between-sunrise-and-sunset calculation cannot be made, such as in the Arctic Circle on a day when sunrise or sunset did not occur, astronomical chatzos may still be used when supported in order to avoid an unavailable time.

Technical details

Source method: ZmanimCalendar.getChatzosHayom

Technical reference: com.kosherjava.zmanim.ZmanimCalendar.getChatzosHayom

Chatzos Hayom As Half Day¶

This is chatzos calculated as halfway between sunrise and sunset. Many are of the opinion that chatzos is the midpoint between sea-level sunrise and sea-level sunset, despite this not being the most accurate way to calculate it. See The Definition of Chatzos for a detailed explanation of ways to calculate chatzos.

It is calculated as the halfway point between sea-level sunrise and sea-level sunset, which is the same as six shaos zmaniyos after sunrise. A day that starts at alos and ends at tzais using the same time or degree offset will also produce the same time.

Because days lengthen or shorten, this is not necessarily the exact midpoint of the day in reality, but it is very close.

This time may not be available when it cannot be calculated, such as in the Arctic Circle on a day when the sun does not rise or does not set.

Technical details

Source method: ZmanimCalendar.getChatzosHayomAsHalfDay

Technical reference: com.kosherjava.zmanim.ZmanimCalendar.getChatzosHayomAsHalfDay
