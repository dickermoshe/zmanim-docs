Alos 120 Minutes¶

Deprecated because this is a very early time and should be used only lechumra, such as stopping eating at this time on a fast day. Using it lekula can result in doing mitzvos hayom too early according to most opinions.

Alos, dawn, according to a very early 120-minute calculation. It is described as suitable only lechumra, such as stopping eating by this time on a fast day, and not as a start time for mitzvos that can only be performed during the day.

Calculated as 120 minutes before sunrise, or before sea-level sunrise depending on the elevation setting. It is based on the time to walk 5 mil at 24 minutes per mil, following the view of the Rishonim that the time between dawn and sunrise depends on walking time rather than season or location.

The time depends on whether elevation is used: it is calculated before sunrise or before sea-level sunrise accordingly.

This zman may not be available where the calculation cannot be computed, such as in the Arctic Circle where there is at least one day a year when the sun does not rise and one when it does not set.

Alos 120 Zmanis¶

Deprecated because this is a very early time and should be used only lechumra, such as stopping eating at this time on a fast day. Using it lekula can result in doing mitzvos hayom too early according to most opinions.

Alos, dawn, according to a very early calculation of 120 zmaniyos minutes, or one-sixth of the day, before sunrise. It is described as suitable only lechumra, such as stopping eating by this time on a fast day, and not as a start time for mitzvos that can only be performed during the day.

Calculated as 120 zmaniyos minutes, equal to one-sixth of the day, before sunrise or sea-level sunrise depending on the elevation setting. It is based on a 24-minute mil, so 5 mil equals 120 minutes, or one-sixth of a 12-hour day. The calculation is also described as sunset minus two GRA seasonal hours.

The day is calculated from sea-level sunrise to sea-level sunset, or from sunrise to sunset, depending on the elevation setting.

This zman may not be available where the calculation cannot be computed, such as in the Arctic Circle where there is at least one day a year when the sun does not rise and one when it does not set.

Technical reference: com.kosherjava.zmanim.ComprehensiveZmanimCalendar.getAlos120Zmanis

Alos 18 Degrees¶

Alos, dawn, when the sun is 18° below the eastern geometric horizon before sunrise.

Calculated by the sun’s position at 18° below the eastern geometric horizon before sunrise.

This zman may not be available in northern and southern locations, even south of the Arctic Circle and north of the Antarctic Circle, where the sun may not reach low enough below the horizon for this calculation.

Alos 19 Degrees¶

Alos, dawn, when the sun is 19° below the eastern geometric horizon before sunrise. This is described as the Rambam’s alos according to Rabbi Moshe Kosower’s Maaglei Tzedek, page 88, Ayeles Hashachar Vol. I, page 12, Yom Valayla Shel Torah, Ch. 34, p. 222, and Rabbi Yaakov Shakow’s Luach Ikvei Hayom.

Calculated by the sun’s position at 19° below the eastern geometric horizon before sunrise.

This zman may not be available in northern and southern locations, even south of the Arctic Circle and north of the Antarctic Circle, where the sun may not reach low enough below the horizon for this calculation.

Alos 19 Point 8 Degrees¶

Alos, dawn, using a degree-based version of the 90-minutes-before-sunrise calculation.

Calculated when the sun is 19.8° below the eastern geometric horizon before sunrise. This is based on the sun’s position 90 minutes before sunrise in Jerusalem around the equinox / equilux, which is calculated as 19.8° below geometric zenith.

This zman may not be available in northern and southern locations, even south of the Arctic Circle and north of the Antarctic Circle, where the sun may not reach low enough below the horizon for this calculation.

Alos 60 Minutes¶

Alos, dawn, calculated as 60 minutes before sunrise. It is based on the time to walk 4 mil at 15 minutes per mil.

Calculated as 60 minutes before sunrise, or before sea-level sunrise depending on the elevation setting. Time-based offset calculations are based on the view of the Rishonim that the time between dawn and sunrise depends on the time it takes to walk 4 mil, rather than the time of year or location.

The time depends on whether elevation is used: it is calculated before sunrise or before sea-level sunrise accordingly.

The 15-minute mil is associated here with the Chavas Yair in Mekor Chaim, Orach Chaim Ch. 90, while other cited sources discuss an 18-minute mil or related views.

This zman may not be available where the calculation cannot be computed, such as in the Arctic Circle where there is at least one day a year when the sun does not rise and one when it does not set.

Alos 72 Zmanis¶

Alos, dawn, calculated as 72 zmaniyos minutes, or one-tenth of the day, before sunrise. This calculation is used in calendars published by the Hisachdus Harabanim D'Artzos Habris Ve'Canada.

Calculated as 72 zmaniyos minutes before sunrise, equal to one-tenth of the day. It is based on an 18-minute mil, so 4 mil equals 72 minutes. The day is based on sea-level sunrise to sea-level sunset, or sunrise to sunset, depending on the elevation setting; the calculation is also described as sunrise based on the elevation setting minus 1.2 GRA seasonal hours.

The time depends on whether elevation is used: the day is calculated from sea-level sunrise to sea-level sunset, or from sunrise to sunset accordingly.

This zman may not be available where the calculation cannot be computed, such as in the Arctic Circle where there is at least one day a year when the sun does not rise and one when it does not set.

Alos 90 Minutes¶

Alos, or dawn, is set as a fixed 90 minutes before sunrise. This is based on the time to walk 4 mil at 22.5 minutes per mil, following the view of the Rishonim that the neshef, the time between dawn and sunrise, does not vary by season or location but depends on the time needed to walk 4 mil.

Calculated as 90 minutes before sunrise, or before sea-level sunrise, depending on the elevation setting.

The time may not be available where the calculation cannot be made, such as in the Arctic Circle where there is at least one day a year when the sun does not rise and one when it does not set.

The calculation depends on whether elevation is used.

Alos Baal Hatanya¶

The Baal Hatanya's alos, or dawn, is the time when the sun is 16.9° below the eastern geometric horizon before sunrise. It is based on 72 minutes between dawn and netz amiti, the time to walk 4 mil at 18 minutes per mil, associated with Rambam and others.

Calculated by the sun’s position: 16.9° below the geometric horizon before sunrise. This angle is based on the sun’s position 72 minutes before netz amiti in Jerusalem around the equinox / equilux.

The time may not be available in northern and southern locations, even south of the Arctic Circle and north of the Antarctic Circle, where the sun may not reach low enough below the horizon for this calculation.
