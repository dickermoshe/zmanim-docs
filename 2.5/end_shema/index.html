Sof Zman Shma 3 Hours Before Chatzos¶

The latest time to recite Shema in the morning, calculated as three regular clock hours before chatzos hayom. This is generally known as part of the “Komarno” zmanim after Rav Yitzchak Eizik of Komarno, though the source text says it predates him and lists earlier and later sources.

Three regular clock hours, not shaos zmaniyos, before chatzos hayom.

The time may not be available or cannot be calculated in places such as the Arctic Circle, where there is at least one day a year when the sun does not rise and one when it does not set.

Technical details

Source method: ComprehensiveZmanimCalendar.getSofZmanShma3HoursBeforeChatzos

Technical reference: com.kosherjava.zmanim.ComprehensiveZmanimCalendar.getSofZmanShma3HoursBeforeChatzos

Sof Zman Shma Alos 16 Point 1 To Sunset¶

The latest time to recite Shema in the morning according to the opinion that the day starts at alos when the sun is 16.1° below the horizon and ends at sea-level sunset. This is cited as the opinion of חידושי וכללות הרז״ה and מנורה הטהורה, as mentioned in Yisrael Vehazmanim vol. 1, sec. 7, ch. 3 no. 16.

Three shaos zmaniyos are calculated from a day that runs from alos 16.1° to sea-level sunset, and are added to alos.

Based on this calculation, chatzos will not be at midday.

The setting for using astronomical chatzos for other zmanim is ignored for this calculation.

The time may not be available or cannot be calculated in northern and southern locations, even south of the Arctic Circle and north of the Antarctic Circle, where the sun may not reach low enough below the horizon for this calculation.

Technical details

Source method: ComprehensiveZmanimCalendar.getSofZmanShmaAlos16Point1ToSunset

Technical reference: com.kosherjava.zmanim.ComprehensiveZmanimCalendar.getSofZmanShmaAlos16Point1ToSunset

Sof Zman Shma Ateret Torah¶

The latest time to recite Shema in the morning based on the calculation of Chacham Yosef Harari-Raful of Yeshivat Ateret Torah.

The day starts 1/10th of the day before sunrise and usually ends 40 minutes after sunset, though that sunset offset is configurable. The time is three Ateret Torah shaos zmaniyos after alos 72 zmaniyos.

Based on this calculation, chatzos will not be at midday.

The time depends on the Ateret Torah sunset offset setting.

The time may not be available or cannot be calculated in places such as the Arctic Circle, where there is at least one day a year when the sun does not rise and one when it does not set.

Sof Zman Shma Baal Hatanya¶

The latest time to recite Shema in the morning according to the Baal Hatanya, based on a day calculated from sunrise to sunset.

Three Baal Hatanya shaos zmaniyos after netz amiti, sunrise.

The time may not be available or cannot be calculated in places such as the Arctic Circle, where there is at least one day a year when the sun does not rise and one when it does not set.

Sof Zman Shma GRASunrise To Fixed Local Chatzos¶

The latest time to recite Shema in the morning according to Rav Moshe Feinstein’s calculation of the GRA opinion that the day is calculated from sunrise to sunset, using only the first half of the day.

The half-day starts at sunrise and ends at fixed local chatzos. Sof zman Shema is three shaos zmaniyos after sunrise, which is half of this half-day.

The time may not be available or cannot be calculated in northern and southern locations, even south of the Arctic Circle and north of the Antarctic Circle, where the sun may not reach low enough below the horizon for this calculation.

Sof Zman Shma MGA 120 Minutes¶

The latest time to recite Shema in the morning according to the Magen Avraham (MGA), based on alos being 120 minutes, or ⅙th of the day, before sunrise. The source describes this as an extremely early zman and very much a chumra.

The day is calculated from dawn 120 minutes before sunrise to nightfall 120 minutes after sunset. The time is three shaos zmaniyos after that dawn.

The time may not be available or cannot be calculated in places such as the Arctic Circle, where there is at least one day a year when the sun does not rise and one when it does not set.

Technical details

Source method: ComprehensiveZmanimCalendar.getSofZmanShmaMGA120Minutes

Sof Zman Shma MGA 16 Point 1 Degrees¶

The latest time to recite Shema in the morning according to the Magen Avraham (MGA), based on alos when the sun is 16.1° below the horizon before sunrise.

The day is calculated from dawn to nightfall, with both dawn and nightfall defined as the sun being 16.1° below the horizon before sunrise or after sunset. The time is three shaos zmaniyos after dawn.

The time may not be available or cannot be calculated in northern and southern locations, even south of the Arctic Circle and north of the Antarctic Circle, where the sun may not reach low enough below the horizon for this calculation.

Sof Zman Shma GRA¶

The latest time to recite Shema in the morning according to the GRA.

It is 3 shaos zmaniyos, or solar hours, after sunrise. The day is calculated from sunrise to sunset, or from sea level sunrise to sea level sunset, depending on the elevation setting.

This time may depend on whether the elevation setting is used.

This time may not be available when the calculation cannot be computed, such as in the Arctic Circle where there is at least one day a year when the sun does not rise and one when it does not set.

Technical details

Source method: ZmanimCalendar.getSofZmanShmaGRA

Technical reference: com.kosherjava.zmanim.ZmanimCalendar.getSofZmanShmaGRA
